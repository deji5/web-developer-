Nigeria Government

About Nigeria Government

The Nigerian government is a federal presidential republic, following the model of the United States, with a strong executive president, a bicameral National Assembly, and a judiciary. Key challenges for the current administration under President Bola Tinubu include improving security, tackling corruption, and enacting economic reforms such as foreign exchange market liberalization and fuel subsidy reform, which have shown some early positive effects. Nigeria also faces ongoing issues with weak public finances, poverty, and deficits in democratic accountability, although there are positive signs like the recent election challenge being handled through judicial channels.

Structure of the Government

  • Executive Branch: The President serves as both the chief of state and head of government, assisted by a Federal Executive Council composed of representatives from all 36 states.
  • Legislative Branch: The National Assembly is a bicameral legislature with a Senate (109 members) and a House of Representatives (360 members).
  • Judicial Branch: The judiciary includes the Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, and Federal High Court, with the President appointing judges subject to Senate confirmation.
